New Delhi: The Reserve Bank of India constituted an Internal Working Group (IWG) to review existing ownership guidelines and corporate structure for the private sector banks. The central bank said that the IWG will examine and review the extant licensing and regulatory guidelines relating to ownership and control, promoters, holding, requirement of dilution, control and voting rights, among others. The review would provide an opportunity to harmonise the norms applicable to banks set up at different time periods, irrespective of their date of commencement of business, RBI said. Prasanna Kumar Mohanty, Sachin Chaturvedi, Lily Vadera, SC Murmu and Shrimohan Yadav are the members of the Working Group. The committee will also examine and review the eligibility criteria for individuals or entities to apply for a banking license and make recommendations on all related issues. It will also study the current regulations on holding of financial subsidiaries through a non-operative financial holding company (NOFHC) and suggest the manner of migrating all banks ..
